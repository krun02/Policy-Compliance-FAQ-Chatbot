Access Control Policy

1. Access to systems and data must be based on the principle of least privilege.
2. HIPAA: Users must be assigned a unique user ID and access should be logged and monitored (HIPAA 164.312(a)(1)).
3. SOC 2: Logical access controls must be in place to restrict access based on roles and responsibilities.
4. NIST SP 800-53 (AC-2, AC-6): Access must be controlled via RBAC, reviewed periodically, and revoked promptly upon role changes or termination.
5. Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for administrative and remote access.
6. Default passwords must be changed during initial system setup.
